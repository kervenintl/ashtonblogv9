2021 - Present | CEO at L3Atom

I launched the world's first open-source crypto data initiative in 2021, called Project L3 Atom. An ambitious project to solve one of the biggest problems in the crypto industry, the unreliable and noisy data problem. L3 Atom recreates the clean L3 order book from its atomic structure in real-time, enabling a new generation of quantitative finance and algorithm trading in financial engineering applications. The historical and live data provided by L3 Atom are free for everyone.


2020 - Present | CEO at Deeplink

I launched an Ethereum-based deep-learning protocol called Deeplink in 2020, which allows blockchains to maintain Large Neural Networks on-chain while introducing programmability, machine learning, and on-chain data science into the blockchain. It will be the first of its kind, offering on-chain neural networks and dev-ops for countless applications. With an SDK, developers can integrate powerful deep-learning tools located on-chain into their smart contracts, providing the perks of deep learning without relying on any centralized authority, and making their products more innovative and safer.

Dr. Booth (2004) - Dr. Booth is a mobile medicare unit with an automated system for delivering drugs and a low-frequency communication system for patients during the 2004 tsunami. This was created for people who were ravaged during the 2004 tsunami. Due to no transportation, food, medicine, and communication for days. I installed a landline and car battery with an antenna on top of the portable booths and called it Dr. Booth. Dr. Booth serves as the phone booth. It helped the people who were stuck in small villages to communicate with their loved ones and also to get medicine.


Smart Traffic Lights (2000) - Smart Traffic Lights consist of pairs of photosensors and logic circuits designed with Boolean logic to help local Sri Lanka police to manage intersection control traffic. It is designed to track how many are on the road to give way to which side has more cars to prioritize. There are sensors that are connected to traffic lights to assess which lane to go first.


Astrology Watch (2000) - Personalized events feedback application based on astrology. This watch predicts your future based on Astrology. With your birthday, month, and time, it calculates your planets and gives you astrological predictions. Displaying outputs and inputting details is done using LED diodes.


Smart House (1999) - I have created a system to control equipment, doors, and lights via voice commands whenever I'm inside the house. Three pairs of photosensors are embedded inside and outside the door, placed at a specific height parallel to my body build. The additional security feature is an early version of the cicret bracelet I wear to send a signal to another pair of photosensors hidden at a specific height of the door.


Wool Cone Microphone (1998) - Low cost FM microphone made of wool cones. I have created a Microphone out of Wool Cones for my school as they have only one microphone.
